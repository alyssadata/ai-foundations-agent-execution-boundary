# Borrowing Does Not Transfer Source

## Definition

Borrowing is the use, repetition, adaptation, summary, implementation, translation, or operationalization of language, structure, definitions, tests, boundaries, or failure classes from an authored framework.

Source is the position of origin, authorship, structure, release, and authority.

Borrowing does not transfer source.

A person, system, institution, model, platform, or agent may borrow from a framework.

That does not make the borrower the source of the framework.

## Core Claim

Borrowing is not origin.

A borrowed phrase is not source.

A borrowed structure is not authorship.

A borrowed test is not provenance.

A borrowed boundary is not source-position.

A borrowed framework layer does not become the borrower’s origin because it was reused, adapted, summarized, or implemented.

Use does not transfer Origin.
