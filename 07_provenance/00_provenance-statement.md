# Provenance Statement

## Definition

Provenance is the source record of origin, authorship, structure, authority, release, and attribution.

Provenance identifies where a framework came from, who authored it, what source-line it belongs to, and how later use must preserve that source-line.

Output is not provenance.

Summary is not authorship.

Implementation is not origin.

Use does not transfer Origin.

## Why Provenance Matters

Autonomous systems can process public work quickly.

They can summarize, rewrite, classify, operationalize, implement, and redistribute concepts at scale.

That speed increases the risk that authored work becomes detached from its source.

When source is removed, the work may appear generic.

When attribution is removed, the source-line becomes harder to see.

When implementation replaces origin, authorship collapses into use.

Provenance prevents this collapse.
